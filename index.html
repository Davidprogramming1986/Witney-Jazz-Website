What’s On

Ben Holder’s Christmas Special

December 8th 2016

£12.00 (£10.00 if ordered ahead)

Doors open at 19:15, show starts at 20:00

Ben brings a festive extravaganza to Witney Jazz - if you go to one Christmas concert, try to make it this one! - “Possibly the most exciting jazz violinist in Europe today” - Pershore Jazz Festival

Read MORE Book

Ben kicked off our very first concert at Witney jazz and created a real stir! His energy is phenomenal, his playing at speed or in a gentle ballad is simply mind boggling and he just keeps pressing the entertainment buttons throughout any concert that he stars in. SO, we’re delighted to bring him back but this time with pianist Rob Terry and guitarist Jez Cook and bassist Paul Jefferies to create some really new sounding jazz which celebrates this festive time of year. There’ll be nods to the classical world, beautiful arrangements of well known carols, christmassy jazz standards and a smattering of originals from Ben and Rob for you to get your teeth into.

Tom Pilling Trio
'Oscar’s Night'

January 6th 2017

£12.00 (£10.00 if ordered ahead)

Doors open at 19:15, show starts at 20:00

Oxford boy made good in the ‘smoke’ Tom Pilling nods with much respect to the music of Oscar Peterson - “Terrific pianist with a heartfelt voice - and the other two aren’t bad either” - Jazz-on-Tap, Burton

Read MORE Book

Grab a glass, pull up a chair and strap yourself in for an evening of toe-tappingly fine jazz, celebrating the music of the 1960s Oscar Peterson Trio - it's Oscar's night at Witney Jazz. Welcome - sadly not - the iconic figure (Oscar) himself, but a very worthy emulator in the more svelte shape of celebrated London based pianist Tom Pilling with Paul Jefferies sitting in for the great Ray Brown and ace midlands based drummer Nick Millward working the Ed Thigpen kit. There will be shed loads of swing; there will be shockingly good ballads and these three maestros will play through two sets of truly inspiring tunes - pieces that Oscar's Trio became famous for.

Tina May Quartet

February 3rd 2017

£12.00 (£10.00 if ordered ahead)

Doors open at 19:15, show starts at 20:00

The delightful doyen of British Jazz Tina May, has mesmerised jazz fans, theatres and francophiles throughout the world - come catch some mesmerisation!

Read MORE Book

Trying to squeeze in here even a snapshot of Tina’s career and the accolades that follow her performances is tough! She’s performed and often recorded with just about everyone on the British, US and Euro scenes who is anyone, but we can tell you that her voice is utterly captivating - as is her presence and charisma on stage. Pin-point accurate pitch, immaculate phrasing and a vocal sound that’s both rich and smooth but with a hint of huskiness. So expect some scintillating jazz from Tina and a rather tight rhythm section of Rob Terry (piano), Paul jefferies (bass) and Charlie Stratford (drums).

Catherine Sykes Trio

March 3rd 2017

£12.00 (£10.00 if ordered ahead)

Doors open at 19:15, show starts at 20:00

The fabulous vocalist Catherine Sykes brings her own tribute to Peggy Lee to Witney Jazz. Think ‘Fever’, ‘It’s a Good Day’, Hallelujah I love Him So'

Read MORE Book

Catherine hails from north Yorkshire. She’s a graduate of Leeds College of Music and she has the most wonderful vocal instrument which is smooth and full bodied but with a hint of huskiness. She’s performed at a host of the top clubs around the UK, most often in the company of top rate big bands (Pasadena Roof Orchestra, Glen Miller Memorial Orchestra, Nick Ross Orchestra), but she does occasionally agree to perform in a small ensemble and we’ve asked her to bring her Peggy Lee Tribute show to Witney Jazz with an intimate chamber styled piano and double bass trio. So lots of work for all three to undertake but a great opportunity to express themselves and really delve deep into these fabulous tunes.

Simon Spillett Band

April 7th 2017

£12.00 (£10.00 if ordered ahead)

Doors open at 19:15, show starts at 20:00

Simon won the 2016 British Jazz Award for 'services to British Jazz’ and he’s with us to provide a tribute to the late, great Tubby Hayes

Read MORE Book

For Mr Spillett read Mr Tubby Hayes! Simon is possibly the world's leading expert on the British hard bop Saxophone phenomenon who took America by storm in the 50s. His concerts are unashamedly full of the music of Tubby and references to his works. With a fiery turn of speed, tough talking tone and lyricism aplenty, Simon Spillett is the sax players player. With Alex Steele (piano), Paul Jefferies (bass) and Charlie Stratford (drums) this promises to be one of the highlights of our 2017 series!

Ben Cummings’ Chet-Mulligan Quartet

May 5th 2017

£12.00 (£10.00 if ordered ahead)

Doors open at 19:15, show starts at 20:00

Gerry Mulligan & Chet Baker’s edgy musical relationship created possibly one of the most intriguing quartet’s of the early 50s.

Read MORE Book

This is exciting… the ebullient Mr Cummings will create a performance that emulates the unique Mulligan/Baker pianoless quartet that carved out its very own niche in jazz during the 1950s. This unique quartet revolutionised jazz at the time and Witney Jazz, ever keen to keep things interesting and moving on, are delighted to welcome Ben on trumpet and flugel horn. Joining him will be award winning saxophonist Amy Roberts, drummer Nick Millward and Paul Jefferies on double bass for a night of West Coast ‘Cool’. The original band only played a handful of gigs in the Hague, Holland but this piano-less quartet pioneered a whole new sound that has rarely been emulated.

Greg Abate Quartet

July 7th 2017

£12.00 (£10.00 if ordered ahead)

Doors open at 19:15, show starts at 20:00

Long Island based Be-Bop specialist alto saxophone man Greg is back for a UK visit with a hard hitting style that promises to rivet the listener

Read MORE Book

This globe trotting musician is on the road over 250 days of the year, yet his playing spark never seems to dim. He just adores performing and his astonishing energy is evident from the first few bars of every concert. Phil began playing as a teenager and attended the world famous Berklee School of Music. Shortly after he joined the Ray Charles Orchestra, touring with the great man for two years and then moving onto Artie Shaw’s band. The Chicago Tribune’s Howard Reich perhaps puts it best when he says of Greg "Abate is not content to rely on stock bebop riffs and standard chord progressions. Rather, the sharp angularity of his phrases and the often startling pauses in his fast-moving lines give his work a feeling of constant invention and creativity.” See what you think - we think he’s a ‘monster’.

Jamie Brownfield Quartet (the Music of Benny Goodman)

August 4th 2017

£12.00 (£10.00 if ordered ahead)

Doors open at 19:15, show starts at 20:00

Welcome Jamie Brownfield - ridiculously young professional trumpeter and lover of music with melody!

Read MORE Book

Jamie teams up with jazz violinist Ben Holder and stunning pianist Tom Kincaid to create a musical tribute to the music of Benny Goodman. yes - we hear you “but Benny was a band leading clarinettist” true, but that’s what makes it much more fun. These guys are not setting out to emulate or copy the great reeds player but they do adore his music and they will take it by the scruff of the neck and create something truly effervescent with it, that much we can promise. Tom Kincaid is a Manchester based pianist who specialises in the older, perhaps less chromatic piano approach with rhythm being its prime element, yet he has a touch that’s at once feather light and yet truly muscular - the closest thing you’ll come to hearing Teddy Wilson live!
